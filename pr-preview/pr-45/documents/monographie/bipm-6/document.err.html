<p id="_596644e8-8119-a083-3076-6258637c5496">As previously mentioned in <xref target="mechanisms"/>, the glass surface is not smooth on a microscale. All glass is porous at the <asciimath>0.1 "unitsml(nm)"</asciimath> level (<eref type="inline" bibitemid="adams2" citeas="Adams, 1972"/>). The porosity of glass that has been leached to selectively remove certain elements from the surface can increase to a depth of <asciimath>1 "unitsml(nm)"</asciimath> to <asciimath>10 "unitsml(nm)"</asciimath> (<eref type="inline" bibitemid="adams2" citeas="Adams, 1972"/>). Therefore, the phenomenon of sorption of radioactive ions can occur when the solution is stored in sealed-glass ampoules.</p>
