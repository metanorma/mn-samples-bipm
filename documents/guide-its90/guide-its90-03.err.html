<title id="_2db4ebd9-af0a-cdc3-2c5b-94daa606fd6b">Primary standards and transducers for pressure measurements</title>
<p id="_5f5b1509-e613-ebfc-12b4-ad1118a486ed">Pressure measurements are required for the realization of the helium vapour-pressure scales, the boiling points of hydrogen and the interpolating gas thermometer, see ITS-90 Guide Chapter 4 <em>Gas Thermometry</em>. <xref target="tab1"/> summarises helium and hydrogen vapour-pressure data and allows themeasurement requirements to be calculated. It shows that in order to cover the complete range for <sup>4</sup>He, it is necessary to measure absolute pressures from <asciimath>100 "unitsml(Pa)"</asciimath> to <asciimath>200 "unitsml(kPa)"</asciimath>, with standard uncertainties of <asciimath>0.1 "unitsml(Pa)"</asciimath> to <asciimath>15 "unitsml(Pa)"</asciimath> (relative <asciimath>0.1 %</asciimath> to <asciimath>75 "unitsml(ppm)"</asciimath>, <asciimath>"unitsml(ppm)"</asciimath> means parts per million), respectively, to achieve <asciimath>0.1 "unitsml(mK)"</asciimath> uncertainty in <asciimath>ii(T)_{90}</asciimath>. The needed relative uncertainties are less wide ranging, varying from <asciimath>30 "unitsml(ppm)"</asciimath> for hydrogen at <asciimath>20.3 "unitsml(K)"</asciimath> to <asciimath>0.1 %</asciimath> for <sup>3</sup>He at <asciimath>0.65 "unitsml(K)"</asciimath>. For gas thermometry, the range is <asciimath>30 "unitsml(ppm)"</asciimath> at <asciimath>3 "unitsml(K)"</asciimath> to <asciimath>4 "unitsml(ppm)"</asciimath> at <asciimath>25 "unitsml(K)"</asciimath>. A measuring instrument with a constant relative uncertainty is thus more suitable than one with a constant absolute uncertainty.</p>

<p id="_bb35b6d3-bef9-8237-76ad-94fd1757f934">Compared with the high-level realizations of the pressure scale, the requirements (in terms of room temperature capability for pressure measurement) of vapour-pressure thermometry are not overly stringent. In the pressure range of interest here, the primary pressure standards of the national metrological institutes are based on liquid-column manometers and pressure balances. Their typical relative uncertainties range from <asciimath>100 "unitsml(ppm)"</asciimath> at <asciimath>100 "unitsml(Pa)"</asciimath> to a few <asciimath>"unitsml(ppm)"</asciimath> at <asciimath>200 "unitsml(kPa)"</asciimath> [<eref type="inline" bibitemid="Miiller2002" citeas="[34]"> <display-text>Miiller <em>et al.</em> 2002</display-text> </eref>, <eref type="inline" bibitemid="Pavese2013" citeas="[40]"> <display-text>Pavese and Molinar Min Beciet 2013</display-text> </eref>].
